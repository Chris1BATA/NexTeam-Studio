# Dive Factor Underwater Services - B2C Strategy

## Primary B2C segments

1. lakefront homeowners
2. retirees and second-home owners
3. premium Keowee and Hartwell waterfront owners
4. family boaters
5. pontoon, wake boat, and fishing boat owners
6. Airbnb and VRBO lake-house owners
7. coastal boat and yacht owners after separate review

## Likely buying triggers

- boat performance concern
- visible dock or lift concern
- storm aftermath
- pre-purchase caution
- missing item recovery need
- swim-area safety concern
- seasonal property reopening
- second-home owner wanting eyes below the waterline

## Messaging themes

- peace of mind below the waterline
- visual proof before guessing
- documented condition, not vague suspicion
- local, practical, professional underwater help
- protect boats, docks, lifts, and waterfront property

## Phase-one strategy requirements

- lead with inspection and documentation, not broad marine-service claims
- keep Hartwell and Keowee as the first geographic focus
- center use cases around boats, docks, lifts, swim areas, storms, and second-home peace of mind
- treat coastal owners as a later branch pending operational review

## Offer direction for homeowners

Best B2C first offer:

- below-waterline inspection for boats, docks, lifts, and swim areas

Possible future recurring offers:

- seasonal waterfront watch plan
- pre-summer opening inspection
- post-storm inspection
- second-home periodic documentation plan

## Content assets needed later

- homepage value proposition draft
- first-offer explainer
- before/after or issue-example library if proof becomes available
- FAQ addressing what the inspection includes and excludes
- trust block focused on professionalism and process, not unverified compliance claims

## B2C guardrails

- do not promise structural certification
- do not promise insurance acceptance
- do not use Aquatrace leak-detection language
- do not imply repairs are included unless they truly are
- do not imply every issue is visible in all water conditions
