# Dive Factor Underwater Services - Phase One Website Copy Direction

## Purpose

Guide internal-only phase-one website copy while staying inside the approved safety boundary.

## Approved positioning

- primary market order: Hartwell first, Keowee second
- posture: visual/documentation-led underwater services only
- tone: practical, clear, calm, and confidence-building

## Approved wording themes

- below-waterline check
- underwater photo/video documentation
- visual inspection
- underwater check
- dock and lift visual check
- swim-area scan
- service request
- written summary

## Wording to avoid

- OSHA compliant
- commercial diving contractor
- certified structural inspection
- engineer-approved
- guaranteed emergency response
- salvage contractor
- final public price claims

## Message priorities

1. explain what the service helps the customer see
2. explain what types of assets are a fit
3. explain that the output is visual documentation and a written summary
4. explain that some services remain subject to review and not everything is offered

## Phase-one homepage direction

- lead with the visibility problem: owners cannot see what is happening below the surface
- present Dive Factor as a practical underwater visual-check and documentation service
- emphasize boats, docks, lifts, swim areas, and waterfront property

## Market-page direction

- Hartwell page should stress broad boating activity, marinas, docks, lifts, and family lake use
- Keowee page should stress premium waterfront ownership, second-home peace of mind, and higher-trust reporting

## Service-page direction

- anchor the site around the Below-Waterline Inspection
- keep lost item recovery secondary
- keep marina partner support positioned as a planning-track or support track, not a public promise-heavy offer

## Pricing direction

- do not publish final public prices
- if any pricing mention is needed later, it should stay broad and approval-gated
