# Dive Factor Camp & Youth Aquatic Programs

Internal-only planning document. Not approved for public sale, publication, outreach, or external claims.

Last updated: 2026-06-15

## Track purpose

Build and evaluate a separate revenue lane under the broader Dive Factor brand for:

- Christian summer camps
- YMCA camps
- private schools
- church youth groups
- lake camps
- retreat centers
- homeschool co-ops
- scout and outdoor groups
- parks and recreation departments

Dive Factor may have a viable seasonal B2B revenue lane built around:

1. Teen introductory scuba experiences in pools or other tightly controlled confined-water settings.
2. Youth snorkeling and underwater-confidence programming.

## Initial offer concepts

### 1. Camp Try Scuba Day

- Audience: teen campers
- Delivery concept: tightly supervised confined-water introductory scuba experience
- Compliance sensitivity: high
- Current posture: concept only, pending NAUI format confirmation

### 2. Youth Underwater Adventure Session

- Audience: youth groups and camps
- Delivery concept: confidence-building pool session that may blend snorkeling, underwater games, and orientation
- Compliance sensitivity: medium to high depending on whether scuba is included
- Current posture: concept only, must separate snorkeling-only options from scuba options

### 3. Camp Staff DAN Safety Training

- Audience: camp staff, lifeguards, waterfront staff, trip leaders
- Delivery concept: DAN-aligned CPR/first aid/AED and oxygen-focused training options
- Compliance sensitivity: medium
- Current posture: strongest near-term internal package candidate

### 4. Camp Aquatic Emergency Readiness Workshop

- Audience: operations leaders and aquatic staff
- Delivery concept: tabletop + equipment + response workflow training
- Compliance sensitivity: medium
- Current posture: good supporting package, but needs a defined incident-readiness scope

### 5. Full Camp Aquatic Safety + Scuba Experience Package

- Audience: camps wanting both camper experience and staff readiness
- Delivery concept: split-day or multi-block package
- Compliance sensitivity: high
- Current posture: bundle concept only until scuba compliance and staffing model are locked

## Source-backed standards snapshot

### NAUI intro scuba facts currently verified

- NAUI Introduction to Scuba is a non-certification introductory program in closely controlled conditions:
  - https://www.naui.org/learn/supervised-diver/introduction-to-scuba-introductory-experienceiso-11121/
  - https://resources.naui.org/media/1580/2024-s-and-p-v10.pdf
- Minimum age is 8 for confined water and 10 for open water in that program:
  - https://www.naui.org/learn/supervised-diver/introduction-to-scuba-introductory-experienceiso-11121/
  - https://resources.naui.org/media/1580/2024-s-and-p-v10.pdf
- Minor participants require signed medical history and waiver/release documents from parent(s) and/or legal guardian(s) before in-water instruction:
  - https://resources.naui.org/media/1580/2024-s-and-p-v10.pdf
- Confined-water instructor ratio is 8:1 under ideal conditions, with more restrictive open-water limits:
  - https://resources.naui.org/media/1580/2024-s-and-p-v10.pdf

### DAN course facts currently verified

- DAN offers Basic Life Support: CPR and First Aid, Emergency Oxygen for Scuba Diving Injuries, and Diving First Aid for Professional Divers:
  - https://dan.org/education-events/training/
- DAN states BLS is open to anyone, even non-divers:
  - https://dan.org/education-events/training/
- DAN states EO2 requires current DAN BLS or other nationally recognized full CPR training as a prerequisite:
  - https://dan.org/education-events/training/
- DAN states DFA Pro includes BLS and EO2 elements and has been accepted by the American Camp Association for staff CPR and first aid requirements:
  - https://dan.org/education-events/training/

## Open safety and compliance questions

1. Which exact NAUI program label should be used for a teen camp pool experience: Introduction to Scuba only, a youth explorer format, or another NAUI-supported structure?
2. Will every camp already provide lifeguards, or must Dive Factor contractually require them?
3. Does Dive Factor's insurance need youth-program, abuse/molestation, participant accident, or additional aquatic endorsements beyond current operations?
4. What staff-to-camper supervision standard should be imposed operationally even if NAUI minimums are met?
5. What minimum age band does Chris actually want to target for phase one: teens only, or younger youth too?
6. What sanitization and shared-gear turnover standard is practical for same-day camp programs?
