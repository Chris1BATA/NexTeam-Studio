# Dive Factor Underwater Services - Market Memo - Lake Keowee

## Question

Does Lake Keowee show enough premium boating, marina, dock, and waterfront-property activity to justify phase-one focus for Dive Factor?

## Sourced findings

- Keowee Marina markets itself as Lake Keowee's largest full-service marina with rentals, boat sales, service, slip rentals, and luxury cottages.
- Sunset Marina positions itself as the first true public marina on Lake Keowee with fueling, docking, rentals, and convenience services.
- Visit Oconee promoted Keowee Marina's April 18-19, 2026 in-water boat show, indicating active local boating demand and event traffic.
- Duke Energy's shoreline management materials confirm that shoreline construction and dock-related changes are regulated on Keowee, which matters for dock/lift-related service conversations.
- Real-estate and lifestyle sources consistently position Keowee as a premium waterfront and second-home market, which supports documentation-oriented, peace-of-mind service angles.

## Internal interpretation

- Keowee looks especially strong for higher-value homeowner and boat-owner positioning.
- The premium-lifestyle angle supports clearer upsell potential for fuller documentation, recurring watch plans, and pre-purchase or second-home monitoring offers.
- Duke shoreline-management rules create a good reason to be precise about scope and avoid structural or permitting claims.

## Dive Factor implications

- Keowee should remain a phase-one launch market alongside Hartwell.
- B2C messaging should emphasize premium waterfront peace of mind, second-home ownership, and visual proof.
- B2B targets should include marinas, brokers, waterfront real-estate channels, and vacation-property partners.

## Assumptions

- Assumption: Keowee's premium property mix could support stronger average-ticket documentation work than some other lake markets.
- Assumption: second-home and luxury-home buyers may place higher value on visual reporting and recurring monitoring.
