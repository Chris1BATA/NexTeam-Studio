# Website Competitive Research Index

Updated 2026-06-16. This index tracks the redesign research used to reposition DiveFactor.com as a more boutique, high-readability static site.

- Required minimum: 25 public pages reviewed per service/page category.
- Research method: curated competitor/service pages, official course/provider pages, and Bing RSS backfill with direct page-title/meta/top-heading review.

## Core Copy Takeaways

- Underwater-service pages win when they explain hidden problems, visible deliverables, and response expectations quickly.
- Dive-training pages often sound generic; Dive Factor should be calmer, more personal, and clearer about who each path fits.
- Camp/group pages perform best when logistics, safety posture, and audience fit are visible without turning into policy manuals.
- Safety-training pages lean on credential acronyms; Dive Factor should translate those into practical readiness benefits.
- Resource pages should feel editorial, not filler: stronger visuals, table of contents, clear read-time cues, and related links matter.
