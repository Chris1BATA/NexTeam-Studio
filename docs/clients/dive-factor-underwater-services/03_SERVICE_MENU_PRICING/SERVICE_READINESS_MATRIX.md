# Dive Factor Underwater Services - Service Readiness Matrix

## Purpose

This matrix separates services that appear ready for planning/package development from services that require further compliance, insurance, operational, or regulatory review.

Planning-ready does not mean public-ready.

| Service idea | Planning status | Why | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Below-Waterline Inspection | likely ready for planning/package development | strongest first offer, visual/documentation-focused, broad fit across boats and waterfront assets | keep scope visual and summary-based |
| Boat hull and running gear visual check | likely ready for planning/package development | clear owner problem, strong inspection fit | no performance guarantee or repair promise |
| Dock and lift visual check | likely ready for planning/package development | strong lake-home and dock-owner use case | no structural certification language |
| Swim-area hazard scan | likely ready for planning/package development | clear family and vacation-home safety use case | describe as visual scan, not guarantee |
| Storm-damage underwater documentation | likely ready for planning/package development | strong trigger event and documentation need | avoid causation or insurance-outcome claims |
| Pre-purchase visual documentation report | likely ready for planning/package development | buyer reassurance use case fits documentation-led model | do not imply marine-surveyor certification |
| Lost item recovery in simple conditions | likely ready for planning/package development | easy-to-understand consumer need | use site and safety screen first |
| Lake-home waterfront watch plans | hold for staged review | recurring offer could be strong but needs scheduling, liability, and scope clarity | phase-two candidate |
| Marina partner plans | hold for staged review | strong B2B potential but needs COI, access, and recurring-service review | phase-two candidate |
| Boat rental fleet inspection plans | hold for staged review | recurring revenue potential but more operational complexity | needs partner workflow review |
| Rope or fishing-line removal in simple conditions | hold for review | can drift into entanglement-risk work quickly | separate safety thresholds needed |
| Heavy rope, line, or entanglement removal | hold for review | elevated diver risk and scope ambiguity | no public offer without operational review |
| Heavy debris removal | hold for review | elevated risk and unclear equipment needs | no public offer |
| Coastal hull cleaning | hold for review | likely higher operational and environmental complexity | separate coastal review required |
| Anode/zinc service | hold for review | maintenance-specific and potentially more technical | do not package yet |
| Structural inspection or certification | hold for review | claim-sensitive and specialist-dependent | not approved |
| Commercial diving contractor positioning | hold for review | regulated-claim risk | not approved |
| Emergency response or salvage | hold for review | high liability and operational burden | not approved |

## Current packaging guidance

- Package around visual documentation first
- Use inspection-led language before maintenance-led language
- Keep B2B recurring plans behind a second-stage review gate
