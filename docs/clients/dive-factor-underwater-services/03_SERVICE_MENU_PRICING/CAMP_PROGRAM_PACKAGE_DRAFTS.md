# Camp Program Package Drafts

Internal-only draft service menu. Not approved for sale, pricing publication, or outreach.

Last updated: 2026-06-15

## Packaging rules

- These are internal draft concepts only.
- Names may change after compliance review.
- No public offer language should be used yet.
- Any scuba-related package is contingent on final NAUI standards alignment and insurance review.

## Package draft 1: Camp Try Scuba Day

### Draft purpose

Give teen campers a closely supervised first scuba experience in a pool or other qualifying confined-water setting.

### Draft fit

- Christian summer camps
- private schools
- retreat centers
- YMCA camps

### Draft delivery shape

- pre-session staff briefing
- participant check-in and paperwork review
- dry safety orientation
- gear fitting
- confined-water intro experience
- post-session debrief and next-step handout for camp leadership

### Internal assumptions

- teen-first positioning is safer than broad youth positioning
- this should stay confined-water-first
- no open-water variant should be packaged in phase one

### Compliance-sensitive notes

- Must stay inside NAUI-approved introductory standards and supervision requirements.
- Minor paperwork must be locked before any in-water participation.
- Camp emergency coverage and supervision structure must be confirmed in writing.

### Current blockers

- exact approved program label
- staffing model
- insurance position
- sanitation SOP

## Package draft 2: Youth Underwater Adventure Session

### Draft purpose

Offer a pool-based confidence and underwater exploration experience that can be scoped as snorkeling-only or as a non-scuba aquatic confidence program.

### Draft fit

- younger camp cohorts
- homeschool groups
- youth groups
- parks and recreation programs

### Internal assumptions

- this may become the lower-liability entry offer if it remains non-scuba
- snorkeling-only and scuba-inclusive versions should never be merged into one vague offer

### Compliance-sensitive notes

- If scuba is included, this becomes a different standards category.
- Age language must stay exact and source-backed.
- Supervision and camp-staff participation rules must be explicit.

### Current blockers

- exact activity scope
- youth age floor decision
- equipment and sanitation model

## Package draft 3: Camp Staff DAN Safety Training

### Draft purpose

Train camp staff in practical emergency response skills relevant to pool, dive-support, and waterfront incidents.

### Draft fit

- camp directors
- aquatics directors
- lifeguard teams
- waterfront staff
- trip leaders

### Draft variants

- BLS-focused session
- BLS + oxygen session
- expanded staff readiness workshop

### Why this is the best first package

- clearer buyer value
- lower youth-program complexity
- useful even when scuba activities are not approved
- easier pilot pathway

### Compliance-sensitive notes

- Must match Chris's actual DAN teaching authority and current credentials.
- Must avoid implying licensing or approval beyond verified DAN course scope.

## Package draft 4: Camp Aquatic Emergency Readiness Workshop

### Draft purpose

Help camps pressure-test their waterfront and pool emergency readiness beyond general CPR training.

### Draft scope ideas

- incident roles and escalation map
- oxygen location and deployment workflow
- EMS activation and meet-point procedure
- missing swimmer or submerged guest response drill
- documentation and post-incident review checklist

### Internal note

This may be stronger as a workshop and SOP-review service than as a certification product.

## Package draft 5: Full Camp Aquatic Safety + Scuba Experience Package

### Draft purpose

Bundle a camper-facing experience with a staff-facing readiness session for camps that want both program value and risk-prep value.

### Draft structure

- block A: staff readiness session
- block B: teen scuba or underwater experience session
- block C: leadership debrief with next-step recommendations

### Internal positioning

- premium package
- by-application only
- only after operational readiness is proven

### Current blockers

- too many moving parts for first launch
- liability stack is materially higher
- should not be the first thing sold

## Recommended package sequence

1. Camp Staff DAN Safety Training
2. Camp Aquatic Emergency Readiness Workshop
3. Camp Try Scuba Day
4. Full Camp Aquatic Safety + Scuba Experience Package

## Offers to avoid for now

- broad "kids scuba camp" language
- mixed-age public drop-in events
- unspecific "certification-ready in a day" phrasing
- any promise that could be read as NAUI or DAN endorsement beyond official standards and course descriptions
