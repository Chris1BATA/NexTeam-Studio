# Camp Program Pricing Framework

Internal-only pricing framework. Not approved for public use or final quote language.

Last updated: 2026-06-15

## Pricing posture

Recommended posture: premium custom pilot pricing with quote-only delivery.

Meaning:
- no public rate sheet yet
- no fixed published per-camper pricing yet
- no "starting at" claims yet
- each opportunity quoted from a structured internal worksheet

## Why this posture fits

- compliance-sensitive delivery
- variable staffing requirements
- variable pool and facility conditions
- variable paperwork/admin burden
- variable insurance and COI requirements
- high brand risk if underpriced and rushed

## Recommended commercial model

### Base structure

- program day fee
- plus participant-volume adjustment where relevant
- plus travel and lodging where relevant
- plus gear transport and sanitation burden
- plus extra safety staffing if required
- plus facility-specific burden if the camp cannot meet minimum readiness requirements

### Minimum quote guardrails

- do not quote youth scuba programs as commodity entertainment
- require a minimum internal margin for admin, paperwork review, setup, and contingency time
- keep bundled packages meaningfully above staff-training-only packages

## Quote inputs

Every quote should account for:

1. Program type
2. Age band
3. Group size
4. Number of in-water participants
5. Number of nonparticipating observers
6. Pool type and depth profile
7. Whether water allows comfortable standing
8. Camp-provided lifeguard coverage
9. Camp-provided supervisory staff
10. Travel distance
11. Gear volume
12. Sanitization turnover burden
13. Paperwork burden
14. Insurance/COI burden
15. Weather backup plan
16. Need for second instructor or qualified assistant

## Internal pricing bands

No numeric public price bands are recommended yet.

Internal approach:
- create bronze, silver, and gold quote templates later
- hold all numbers offline until:
  - insurance review is complete
  - staffing model is locked
  - program duration assumptions are tested
  - actual gear turnaround time is known

## Recommended discount posture

- no broad camp discounting in phase one
- no nonprofit discount promises until margin model is known
- if anything is discounted, discount only for:
  - multi-session bookings
  - staff training + youth experience bundle
  - close-radius low-travel pilots

## Red-line rules

- No pricing should be shown on a website yet.
- No final pricing should be stated in outreach or PDFs.
- No per-student promise should be made until staffing ratios and paperwork friction are validated.
- No package should be sold at a rate that assumes ideal conditions unless the contract defines those conditions.

## Recommended first quote strategy

For the first real opportunities:

- quote staff training first
- quote youth scuba only as a custom pilot
- require operational intake before quoting
- include an explicit note that final scope depends on facility, participant ages, supervision, and paperwork completion
