# Dive Factor Underwater Services - Safety Boundary and Compliance Checklist

## Purpose

This document is a planning boundary, not legal advice.

Its job is to prevent unsafe or unverified public claims while the Dive Factor lane is being built.

## Hard public-claim boundary

Do not publicly claim any of the following unless Chris has verified the underlying proof:

- OSHA compliant
- certified structural inspection
- commercial diving contractor
- engineering-grade inspection
- insurance-approved contractor
- code-certified inspection
- emergency response readiness

## What can be described more safely

These descriptions are lower-risk if kept precise:

- underwater photo and video documentation
- visual check
- visual condition notes
- below-waterline inspection
- swim-area hazard scan
- written summary and referral recommendation

## Service gating

### Likely ready for planning and package design

- underwater photo and video documentation
- visual boat hull and running gear checks
- visual dock and lift checks
- swim-area hazard scans
- storm-damage visual documentation
- pre-purchase visual documentation reports
- simple-condition lost item recovery after site screen

### Hold for review

- any claim of OSHA compliance
- any claim of commercial diving contractor status
- structural inspection or certification language
- repair or remediation promises beyond verified capability
- coastal hull cleaning and anode or zinc service
- recurring maintenance agreements with operational guarantees
- entanglement-heavy rope or fishing-line removal
- heavy debris removal
- salvage, emergency response, or insurance-outcome positioning

## Readiness checklist

### Insurance and business review

- verify general liability scope
- verify any marine or diving-specific exclusions
- verify workers compensation implications if team expands
- verify vehicle, trailer, and equipment coverage
- verify proof of insurance format for marinas and partners

### Diving operations review

- define solo vs. tender-supported operating rules
- define site screen and no-go criteria
- define emergency action plan
- define communications and topside support requirements
- define visibility, current, entanglement, and contamination thresholds
- define training and documentation standards
- define when surface support is mandatory
- define when client-supplied access or dock conditions make a job non-viable

### Service-scope review

- define visual-only language
- define what qualifies as a reportable issue
- define what must be referred out
- define what services stay out of scope

### Documentation review

- standardize photo/video naming
- standardize summary report format
- standardize limitations/disclaimer block
- standardize proof retention
- define what language is forbidden in summaries without specialist review

### Site and access review

- marina permission rules
- HOA or POA access rules
- private property authorization
- boat-owner authorization
- environmental or wildlife restrictions where relevant

## SOP binder outline

1. pre-job intake and scope screen
2. go/no-go safety screen
3. equipment checklist
4. in-water operating procedure
5. media capture standard
6. report-writing standard
7. referral and escalation rules
8. incident and near-miss reporting
9. emergency action plan
10. client communication limitations

## Gating rule

If a service needs legal, insurance, OSHA, environmental, or operational review, mark it as:

- hold for review

Do not move it into approved sales copy until the proof exists.

## Current compliance-sensitive items held for review

- coastal hull cleaning
- anode or zinc service
- marina recurring maintenance plans with service guarantees
- commercial-diving-style contractor positioning
- structural, engineering, or certified inspection language
- emergency or salvage positioning
